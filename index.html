About Us

We are four guys who will be rowing across the Atlantic in the winter of 2018, to raise money for the Berkshire Multiple Sclerosis Therapy Centre.

The North Atlantic Ocean is one of the most challenging, unpredictable and dangerous bodies of water in the world. Atlantic Disovery is going to attempt the gruelling row during December 2018 coinciding with the end of the hurricane season to get the most benefit from the easterly trade winds and Atlantic currents.

Campaign

We will set off from Europe at San Sebastion de la Gomera in Spain, and will not see land until reaching one of the Leeward Islands of the Caribbean, Barbados or Antigua.

The row itself will take between 35-45 days and covers a distance of 4800km (2900 miles).

Equipment

During the row we will carry with us all the food and equipment needed for a successful crossing.

There is no room onboard for home comforts: no bathroom facilities, limited cooking ability and our diet will consist of high calorie expedition foods and snacks. We will drink desalinated water and sleep in a space smaller than a single bed.

Safety

The team has undergone sea survival training, gained their RYA/MCA VHF Short Range Certificate and also their RYA/MCA Yachtmaster Ocean (Shorebased) Certificate.

The boat will carry all the required safety and emergency equipment (EPRIB, liferaft, flares, and survival suit) and the team will be advised regularly on weather routing during the challenge.

Our Cause

We are rowing to raise money for the Berkshire Multiple Sclerosis Therapy Centre.

Multiple Sclerosis has affected our team personally, and MS Therapy Centres receive little statutory or other regular core funding support, depending entirely on donations and fundraising to continue. Securing adequate operational funding remains is crucial to the continuation of the Berkshire Centre. It is expected to cost over £360,000 to run the centre per year.

Our Goals

Research

We are partnering with several research groups in some of the best research universities in the world - including SHARP at UCL.

Charity

We aim to raise £250,000 to continue the operation of the Berkshire Multiple Sclerosis center, to increase disease awareness and knowledge of its cause.

Rowing

We want to win (or at least be in the top three!). In addition, we plan to go after some Concept2 rowing machine records in the training process.
